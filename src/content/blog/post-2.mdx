## One page or many?

One of the most common questions we hear is: "Do I need a full website, or will a single page do the job?" The answer depends entirely on what you're trying to achieve.

### When to choose a landing page

A landing page works best when you have a **single, focused goal**. Common examples include:

- A product launch or promotion
- A lead generation campaign for Google or Meta ads
- An event registration page
- A waitlist for a new service

Landing pages remove distractions and guide visitors toward one specific action — whether that's filling in a form, making a booking, or signing up.

### When you need a full website

A multi-page website is the right choice when visitors need to explore your business before making a decision. You'll want separate pages for:

- Different services or products
- Your company story and values
- Customer testimonials and social proof
- Contact information and location

### Not sure which is right?

That's completely normal. The right structure depends on your goals, your audience, and how people typically find your business. A good first step is a discovery call where we can talk through what you need.
