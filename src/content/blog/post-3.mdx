## Don't hire a web designer without asking these

Finding the right person to build your website can feel overwhelming. Here are five questions that will help you separate the pros from the amateurs.

### 1. Can I see examples of your work?

Any good designer will have a portfolio. Look for variety — if every site looks the same, they might be using templates rather than doing custom work.

### 2. Do you handle hosting and maintenance?

Some designers hand over the files and walk away. Others offer ongoing support. Decide which you need before you start.

### 3. Will my site be mobile-friendly?

This should be a given, but it's worth confirming. A responsive design isn't optional anymore — it's essential.

### 4. What's included in the price?

Get a detailed breakdown. Design, development, content, SEO setup, revisions — know what's covered so there are no surprises.

### 5. What happens after the site launches?

Will they hand over the keys and disappear? Or will they be around for support, updates, and improvements? A website isn't a one-and-done project.
